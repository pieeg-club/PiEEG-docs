# Power Bank

Power Bank
All PiEEG devices must be powered from a battery (power bank) only — not from mains power. This ensures electrical isolation and safe, clean signal acquisition.
Choosing a Power Bank
For PiEEG, PiEEG-16, and JNEEG, use a power bank with a 5V output, a minimum output current of 3A, and a capacity of no more than 10,000 mAh.
For ardEEG, use a smaller power bank: 5V output with no more than 1A output current and a capacity of up to 1,000 mAh.
A single power bank can supply both the PiEEG device and a connected monitor at the same time, as long as it meets the current requirements for your device.
Choosing a USB Cable
Use the shortest USB cable practical for your setup. Longer cables increase resistance and can cause voltage drops that affect device stability. Always use a thick-gauge cable to ensure sufficient current is delivered to the device.

### Power Specifications

| Device | Voltage | Output Current | Capacity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PiEEG** | 5V | 3A – 3.5A | Up to 10,000 mAh |
| **PiEEG-16** | 5V | 3A – 3.5A | Up to 10,000 mAh |
| **JNEEG** | 5V | 3A – 3.5A | Up to 10,000 mAh |
| **ardEEG, MicroBCI** | 5V | Up to 1A | Up to 1,000 mAh |
